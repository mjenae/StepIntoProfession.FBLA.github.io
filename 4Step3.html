Step 3: Completing The Cover Letters, Resumes, and Applications

Now that you have an organized list of what jobs appear to meet your standards, it is important that you prepare to actually obtain one. The 3 necessities of the process aligning you for a clear shot at having an interview and chance to get the job are the cover letter, resume and application supplied by the company.

Cover Letters

The cover letter is a document explaining how and why you wish to acquire the job based on your skillset, which should serve as a good fit for the job opening you've considered. Often times, the letter may be optional; however, if so, completing the letter definitely increases your chances of being considered for the job because the document serves almost as a pitch for, what you hope will be, your upcoming interview. Typically, you'll want to include a contact section (The best options to leave include your cell phone number and appropriate email address), a salutaion (greeting), information exemplifying why you qualify for the job (serving as the body), a professional closing, and your signature...ALWAYS! (Examples of cover letter templates can be easily found as examples are supplied by Microsoft and many other sources.)

Resumes

Before applying to the job of your choice, consider updating your resume. In other words, work to incorporate as much of your past work experience (and what you were responsible for in your position) as possible into one appealing and professionaly organized document. Key components of your resume should include: making sure all of your contact information is up to date, qualities about your work ethic, your past/obtained education and any high lights or certifications you have. (Again, examples are supplied by Microsoft, Resume-Now [powered by LiveCareer] and many other sources)

The Application
The application for any job may either be found online, via email or in person; however, no matter the circumstance, remember to follow the guidelines/directions of the specific applications. Not doing so can hurt your chances of having a chance at an interview. Be sure to include your resume when submitting the application along with your cover letter. Often times, jobs, especially of a higher level, require both to be received at the same time as the application. Furthermore, pay close attention to the reference section, the section employers often look to when trying to learn more about you from your past employers. Be sure to inlcude as much contact information for his or her as possible, especially if they can make positive remarks about your work ethic personality.

You are now officially ready for Step 4!
