Step 4: Nailing the Interview

You've made it past each of the three steps and you are called in for an interview, a face to face meeting with your potential future employer. The only thing left to do is be yourself, remain confident and composed, and stay as professional as possible. You want to make the best impression. Consider the following:

Ladies: Dress to Impress

Ladies should lean more towards an appropriately lengthened dress paired with a blazer or a business suit featuring a blouse with either slacks or an appropriately lengthed skirt. (The dress and skirt are best if they are at least to the knees or fingertips; Another option would be capris or gauchos paired with a coordinating blazer.) Remember to pair the look with dress shoes and appropriate accessories: heels of an appropriate height and flats are two of the best options. Steer away from clothing that may flash body piercings, jeans, shorts (along with any type of excercise clothing), tight items, backless items, sandals, athletic shoes, midriff tops, bathing suits, hats and flannels, and visible foundation garments as these pieces of clothing do not reflect an indivudal's level of professionalism.

Gentlemen: Dress to Impress

Gentlemen should lean towards a business suit with a collard dress shirt and neck tie. (A second option would be sport coat paired with the collard shirt.) Slacks should be warn and paired with appropriate dress shoes and socks. Steer away from clothing that may flash body piercings, jeans, shorts (along with any type of excercise clothing), tight items, backless items, sandals, athletic shoes, midriff tops, bathing suits, hats and flannels, and visible foundation garments as these pieces of clothing do not reflect an indivudal's level of professionalism.

Things to Avoid
The goal of the interview is to get the job! Stay away from badmouthing yourself or the company, rushing yourself and not considering what you will say to the employer, overexagerating, stumbling, lying, sounding weary or unsure, appearing clueless about the company and especially making tacky excuses!.

Tips to Do
Remember, the goal is to impress the employer! Be on time, actually think about what you will wear, speak up, speak clearly, consider what you will say, be a good listener in order to understand the question, and plan/suggest to always follow up!

Now, you are exceptionally capable of getting the job!
