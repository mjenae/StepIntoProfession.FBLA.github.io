Step 2: Beginning the Search

Now that you've got your list, it's time to actually begin the search. Start by researching along the lines of your interests, but remember, a key tip is to remain organized! The search tends to get a bit hectic as you are flowing from one page to the next and inhaling new knowledge about diverse topics. Keep track of all of your pertinent searches in a consistent and manageable mode. (One of the best ways to do so being sectioning or keeping a chart: keep the main information you search for constant but change the company or occupation.)

Start Online

Today's technology has allowed society to progress tremendously, especially easing the search for job openings and company advertising. Companies today often suggest open positions of employment accross the web and include the key requirements and purposes of each job. Society's progression has made it much more likely for you to find a company along the lines of your intersts through simple searches and even computer generated recognition (of your interests [pop ups, etc.]). If you're unfamiliar with companies that may match exactly what you're looking for, go ahead and start with job search engines, which allow you to enter specific key words and even a geographic location. Some of the most reliable include LinkUp.com , Glassdoor.com , and Idealist.com.

Attach to Social Media

An important part of the job selection process is branching out to shocase your potential. With today's technology, the perfect and most efficient way to exhibit your talents to a plethora of companies across the globe is by taking advantage of the numerous social media platforms all over the net. Tools especially similar to LinkedIn.com are speficially designed to give people access to successful companies, fellow employees and peers, and others searching for a job. To paraphrase, the resources are provided for your benefit: to not only assist you in finding the best options, but to also showcase and demonstrate your capabilites to potential employers.

Select Wise Options
Now that you've searched and noted as many potential options for yourself as possible, you'll want to organize your options further. In other words, order your options. What jobs seemed to appeal to you the most? What options are you least likely to choose after observing your entire list? What are the positives and negatives to each option? Remember, you have a list! Frequently look back at the checklist for answers, especially if you find yourself unsure or even confused, but the greatest thing now, is you have options!

You are now officially ready for Step 3!
